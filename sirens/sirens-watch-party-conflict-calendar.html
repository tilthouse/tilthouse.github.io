🏠 Home/Away Nuance — How it affects severity ▾

When Padres or SDFC are home, many fans go to the stadium — fewer at bars, but bars still default to showing the game. When they're away, those fans flood bars and actively demand screens and sound.

On the daily timelines below, home games are faded and away games are solid to reflect this difference.

Either way, any overlapping Padres game — home or away — means you need a venue that doesn't prioritize baseball.

Special case — Chargers: Since the Chargers play in LA, the home/away distinction doesn't apply. SD Chargers fans are always watching on TV at bars.

Moderate — Free streaming, apps for most hardware

Free but streaming-only. Apps available on Apple TV, Roku, Fire TV, Android TV, smart TVs, and web. A venue needs a streaming device connected to their TV system, but no subscription cost.

Hard — Paid subscription streaming, apps for most hardware

Prime VideoESPN+Paramount+

Requires a paid subscription and a streaming device. Apps available on most platforms. Some venues have Amazon Fire TV Sticks already; others will need the Sirens to bring one and log in.

Very Hard — Free streaming, limited platform support

NWSL+

Free but available only on Apple TV, Roku, Fire TV Stick, and web browsers. No apps for Samsung, LG, Vizio, or HiSense smart TVs. Older bars using DirecTV for Business with built-in TV apps cannot access it at all. Typically requires a laptop-to-HDMI connection or bringing a streaming stick.
